## Discussion & decisions

- **Cards** were AI-seeded (via "Vector") plus Sleety's own thinking, spanning People / Money / Process / Reach / Product / Aircraft. Aircraft cards (Quiver, Spearhead, Caribou, Longshot, etc.) were added at the end so a specific build could be named as top priority.
- **thomasg's observation:** almost everyone put Manufacturing AIP / Revenue / Tokenomics in their top 2 — "different flavors of the same thing." Sleety bundles **tokenomics into the manufacturing use case** rather than treating it separately.
- **thomasg swapped Onboarding → Contributor pipeline** mid-session after Sleety made the case that the pipeline is the "glue" — accessibility to tasks + recognition/reward for contributions large or small.
- **GitHub Workflow Improvements** (thomasg's added card — CAD in version control, real-time docs) to be **bundled into Contributor pipeline & bounties**. Making the codebase approachable for a new engineer.
- **Revenue streams** framed as downstream of manufacturing + the devkit launch; more a growth/marketing challenge (Quiver in a state people will pay for). thomasg's top item is the web store / AIP-010 shop, which has revenue baked in.
- **Attachment developer ecosystem** flagged by Sleety as a low-barrier entry point — people who can't build a full Spearhead/Quiver can contribute CAD attachments (envisioned like a 3D-printing marketplace). Overtook contributor pipeline in the live ranking at one point.
- **Manned aircraft roadmap** (KBM #1, thomasg #6): valued as a cultural / mindshare item — a written long-term "dream" north star for onboarding, even if no active engineering. Currently unwritten anywhere. Note it under onboarding.
